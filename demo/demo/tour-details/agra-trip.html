Agra trip 5

Description

Agra is a popular tourist destination as it is home to one of the 7 wonders of the world, the Taj Mahal. It is a sneak peek into the architectural history and legacy of the Mughal empire with two other UNESCO World Heritage Sites Agra Fort and Fatehpur Sikri. History, architecture, romance all together create the magic of Agra, and hence, makes for a must-visit for anyone living in or visiting India.

Agra is one of the most populous cities in Uttar Pradesh and 24th most populous city in India. With its long and rich history, it is no wonder that Agra forms part of the popular Golden Triangle Circuit for tourists along with Delhi and Jaipur. It is also a part of the Uttar Pradesh Heritage Arc including Varanasi and Lucknow. History fanatics and architecture buffs are sure to have a ball here with the sheer expanse of the Mughal art and culture on display.

Apart from its monuments, Agra has some exciting stuff for foodies. It is as famous for its Petha (a sweet made from pumpkin and flavoured with rose water and saffron) as it is for the Taj Mahal. Agra is also well known for its marble artefacts which are best bought in the Sadar Bazaar or Kinaari Bazaar area. 

Agra is mostly visited on a one-day trip from New Delhi or other nearby cities in Uttar Pradesh but is totally worth it. Be prepared to be astounded, amazed, inspired and thrilled. 

Commence your trip from Delhi, and let our representatives take you towards your hotel in Agra. Despite being the single largest tourist attraction in the city, the majesty of Taj Mahal draws visitors to Agra like moths to a wondrous flame. And, let us tell you that the magical allure of this epitome of love is not just a hype. If you do not believe us, let us book a visit to this historical monument for you. Choose from our Agra sightseeing tour packages that suits you best, and let us leave you in this wonderland. To witness the grandeur of Taj Mahal at its best, all you need to do is get up early in the morning. As the rising sun casts golden hues upon the marble, the magnificent monument seems no less than a heaven!

What’s more? Adding to your anticipation, Taj Mahal is not the only attraction to visit in Agra. Thanks to the Mughal Empire, this city has a plethora of marvelous tombs and fascinating forts, like the Agra Fort. And, with fine works of art and craft, a peek-a-boo at its vibrant culture, and sumptuous delicacies to savour, here’s your chance to add some spice to your trip. The best idea to make the most of local sightseeing tour packages in Agra is to step out and explore the city on your own. Visit some famous eateries or restaurants and give your taste buds a tantalizing experience. And, if you are left with ample amount of time, then visit the local markets there to buy some souvenirs, so that before you leave this city you must have a bag of memories to take along.

Detailed Day Wise Itinerary

  • Day : 1 Delhi - Red fort-Sightseeing

    Start journey from Noida or Delhi via The Red Fort or Lal Qila is a historic fort in Old Delhi, Delhi in India that served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors. Emperor Shah Jahan commissioned construction of the Red Fort on 12 May 1638, when he decided to shift his capital from Agra to Delhi.

    Explore the various settings within like the bath area, sleeping room and the various halls where the royalty used to entertain private visitors. Many mahals like the Mumtaz Mahal, Khas Mahal and the Rang Mahal are interesting places to look into. In evening move to Agra and stay night in hotel.

     

    Visitor Information

    • Famous For: History, Beauty, Architecture
    • Entrance Fee: 35 per person for Indians and Rs.500 per person for foreigners
    • Visit Timings: 9:30 AM to 4:30 PM (Daily)
    • Visit Duration: 2 hours to 3 hours
